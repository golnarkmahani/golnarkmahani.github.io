About Me

I am an AI Scientist with over 10 years of experience specialising in the development of AI solutions for complex Healthcare challenges. My work is defined by a commitment to navigating the hurdles of data scarcity and model reliability to build high-impact healthcare solutions that bridge the gap between clinical complexity and computational excellence.

Professional Perspective: Strategic Priorities of AI in Healthcare

The most effective AI solutions are built at the intersection of technical rigour and strategic product vision. My approach is grounded in the reality that in healthcare, a model’s success is measured by its clinical utility. Certified in Agile/Scrum Product Management and Project Management, I have a demonstrated ability to lead cross-disciplinary teams, translating intricate technical requirements into actionable product roadmaps. My goal is to ensure that technical innovation translates directly into better patient outcomes. In delivering AI for high-stakes clinical environments, I am guided by these strategic priorities:

  • A Holistic Approach to Development: While high-accuracy scores are a vital benchmark, I believe AI development in healthcare demands a more comprehensive perspective. I prioritize integrating data quality, ethics, and security from the outset, ensuring that solutions are not only high-performing but also safe, unbiased, and transparent enough to earn clinical trust.
  • Prioritising Data Excellence as The Foundation:In healthcare, data issues manifest as compounding events that cause invisible, negative downstream effects on model reliability. By applying rigorous curation at the source, I work to eliminate these avoidable failures, ensuring the transparent foundations necessary for clinical trust and safer innovation.

CoNILab, Sir Peter Mansfield Imaging Centre, University of Nottingham, Nottingham:

Delivered a speech on the Concept of Topological Data Analysis, 31 Jan 2019:

Abstract: The main goal of Topological Data Analysis (TDA) is to use ideas and results from geometry and topology to develop tools for studying qualitative features of complex data. The topological and geometric information provides new features of the data; and these features can be used for a better understanding of the data by visualisation or can be used in a second stage of analysis using machine learning techniques. In this talk, I introduced the fundamental concepts of TDA and an open discussion on the possible applications of it to NeuroImaging research. Presentation Slides Here
