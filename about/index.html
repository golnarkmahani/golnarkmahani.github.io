About Me

I received my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering, specializing in Software, and pursued an M.Sc. degree in Artificial Intelligence with a keen focus on Medical Data Mining. My research during this period revolved around reducing unnecessary laboratory blood tests in ICU GI bleeding patients, utilizing the MIMIC-II clinical database.

Currently, I am a passionate Doctoral researcher at the University of Nottingham, under the expert guidance of Dr. Andrew P French, Dr. Xin Chen, Dr. Stamatios Sotiropoulos, and Dr. Nikos Evangelou. My research focus is medical image segmentation using deep learning. Over the years, I have gained extensive experience working with challenging datasets such as MIMIC-II, Brain MRI, Breast Ultra Sound, and ISIC2018 skin lesion images. Throughout my academic career, I have developed a strong ability to adapt to a variety of contexts, which has enabled me to quickly comprehend and work with a wide range of data.

My Research Interests Include:

  • Medical Image Analysis
  • Machine Learning
  • Computer Vision
