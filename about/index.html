About Me

Holding a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering with a concentration in Software Development, I advanced my education with a Master's in Artificial Intelligence. My M.Sc. research was uniquely centred on Medical Data Mining, specifically aiming to minimise unnecessary blood tests for ICU patients with GI bleeding. This research was based on the comprehensive MIMIC-II clinical database.

Presently, I am a Doctoral researcher at the University of Nottingham. Under the mentorship of Dr. Andrew P French, Dr. Xin Chen, Dr. Stamatios Sotiropoulos, and Dr. Nikos Evangeloumy research trajectory is directed towards medical image segmentation using the latest deep learning methodologies. Over the span of my academic journey, I have gained considerable proficiency in handling complex datasets and deploying machine learning strategies. This journey has given me the skills to handle different research areas, enabling me to swiftly adjust to and utilise different data sets.

My Research Interests Include:

  • Medical Image Analysis
  • Machine Learning
  • Computer Vision

Academic Experience

CoNILab, Sir Peter Mansfield Imaging Centre, University of Nottingham, Nottingham:

Delivered a speech on the Concept of Topological Data Analysis, 31 Jan 2019:

Abstract: The main goal of Topological Data Analysis (TDA) is to use ideas and results from geometry and topology to develop tools for studying qualitative features of complex data. The topological and geometric information provides new features of the data; and these features can be used for a better understanding of the data by visualisation or can be used in a second stage of analysis using machine learning techniques. In this talk, I introduced the fundamental concepts of TDA and an open discussion on the possible applications of it to NeuroImaging research. Presentation Slides Here.
